SECURE RESELLER ENVIRONMENT

Keyword research is the foundation of everything. Find what your audience actually searches for, match your content to their intent, and build a targeting strategy before writing a single word.

Step 1 — Understand search intent first

Informational

User wants to learn. "What is web design?" — Target with blogs, guides, FAQs. High traffic, lower conversion intent.

Commercial

User is comparing. "Best web design agencies Delhi" — Target with comparison pages. High conversion potential.

Transactional

User wants to hire now. "Hire web designer Delhi" — Target with service pages. Highest intent, build these first.

Navigational

User wants a specific brand. "Webresolv contact" — Optimise your brand name. You can't rank for competitors' brands.

Step 2 — How to find your focus keyword

  • 1
    Seed keywords Start with 5–10 broad terms: "web design", "digital marketing", "SEO services", "website development Delhi". These are your starting points, not your targets.
  • 2
    Expand with tools Plug seeds into Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, or SEMrush. Harvest related keywords, questions, and "People Also Ask" variations. These are real search variations.
  • 3
    Check keyword difficulty (KD) New/small site: target KD 0–30. Established DA 30+ site: up to KD 50. Never target KD 80+ early. You will not rank. Pivot to long-tail variations.
  • 4
    Validate search volume Minimum 50–100/mo for local niche terms. 500–1,000+ for broader terms. A 50/mo transactional keyword beats a 5,000/mo informational one for revenue.
  • 5
    Check the SERP manually Google your keyword. If page 1 is all major brands, pivot to a long-tail. If it's blog posts and small sites, you can compete. The SERP tells you what Google wants.
  • 6
    Spy on competitors Use Ahrefs or SEMrush to see exactly what keywords your competitors rank for. Find gaps — keywords they rank for that you haven't targeted yet.

Short-tail vs long-tail

Short-tail (head keywords)

1–2 words. High volume, high KD, broad intent. "web design" = 50k searches, KD 85. Use sparingly on home/pillar pages only. Hard to rank without strong domain authority.

Long-tail keywords

3+ words. Lower volume, low KD, clear intent. "web design agency small business Delhi" = 200 searches, KD 20. Easy to rank, high conversion. Target these aggressively across all pages.

Free keyword research workflow

  • Google your seed keyword → scrape "People Also Ask" and "Related Searches" at bottom. Free goldmine of exact long-tail phrases people search.
  • Use Google Autocomplete — type your keyword + each letter of the alphabet. Every suggestion is a real search pattern.
  • Check Google Search Console for queries already sending traffic. Find positions 5–20 — those are fastest wins. Improve those pages and push them to page 1.
  • Build a keyword map — assign ONE primary keyword per page + 3–5 secondary/LSI keywords. Never target the same keyword on two pages (keyword cannibalization kills rankings).

Priority formula: Best keywords score high on business value (does this bring paying clients?) + transactional intent + manageable KD for your current domain authority + enough volume to matter. Optimise for revenue, not traffic vanity metrics.

On-page SEO is every element you control directly on the page — from HTML structure to your content. Nail these and you give Google every signal it needs to rank you correctly for the right queries.

Anatomy of a perfectly optimised page

  • Title tag 50–60 chars. Focus keyword near the start. Format: "Focus Keyword | Brand Name". E.g. "Web Design Agency Delhi | Webresolv". Every page needs a unique title — never duplicate.
  • Meta description 150–160 chars. Not a direct ranking factor but controls CTR heavily. Include keyword, clear value prop, call to action. "Delhi's top web design agency. Fast sites, SEO-ready. Get a free quote today."
  • H1 tag One per page only. Contains your focus keyword naturally. This is the page's title as Google reads it. "Affordable Web Design Services in Delhi" — do not repeat the same H1 across pages.
  • H2 / H3 structure H2s are section headers, include secondary keywords. H3s are sub-points within H2 sections. This hierarchy helps Google understand content depth and earns featured snippets.
  • First 100 words Mention your focus keyword in the opening paragraph. Google weights early occurrence heavily. Answer the query or state the topic immediately — don't warm up for 300 words.
  • URL slug Short, lowercase, hyphenated, keyword-included. /web-design-delhi not /page?id=43. Under 60 chars. Never change a live URL without a 301 redirect — you'll lose all its ranking equity.
  • Image alt text Describe every image with keyword-relevant alt text. alt="web design portfolio Delhi 2025". Helps Google index images and contributes to topical relevance signals.
  • Internal links Link to 3–5 related pages within your site using descriptive anchor text (not "click here"). Distributes ranking authority (PageRank) across your site and helps Google discover all your pages.
  • External links Link out to 1–2 authoritative sources (Google, a reputable study, industry org). Signals you've done research and your content is credible. Many people avoid this — don't.
  • Content length Match what already ranks. Check the average word count of page 1 results for your keyword. For competitive queries: 1,500–2,500 words. Local service pages: 800–1,200 can be enough. Quality beats length every time.
  • Keyword density 1–2% is fine. Don't stuff. Use LSI (related) keywords naturally throughout. Google's language models understand synonyms and context — write naturally for humans, not keyword bots.

LSI keywords: Terms Google associates with your main keyword. For "web design Delhi", LSI includes: UI/UX design, responsive website, mobile-friendly, website development India, WordPress developer, landing page design. Use these naturally — they signal topical authority and help you avoid over-optimising one exact phrase.

Content quality checklist (E-E-A-T)

  • Answers the full query — if someone searches "web design cost India", your page must actually give them real numbers
  • 100% original — never copy competitor content. Google's Helpful Content System penalises duplicate/scraped content with site-wide ranking drops
  • Refresh every 6–12 months — update dates, stats, examples. Fresh content signals relevance for time-sensitive queries
  • E-E-A-T signals — author bio with credentials, real client results, case studies, a photo of you. Google trusts a real expert over an anonymous page

Schema markup is code you add to pages that tells Google exactly what your content means — not just the words, but the entity. It unlocks rich results in SERPs that dramatically increase your click-through rate without needing to rank higher.

Why this matters for your agency: With schema, your result can show star ratings, FAQ dropdowns, business hours, pricing, and more — taking 3× the SERP real estate. More visibility → more clicks → better CTR signal → better rankings. It's a compounding advantage.

Schema types you need for a web design agency

LocalBusiness / Org

Name, address, phone, hours, logo, socials. Appears in Google's Knowledge Panel. Essential for local search rankings.

Service

Describes your services explicitly — name, description, provider, area served. Helps Google categorise what you offer.

FAQPage

FAQ sections expand directly in SERPs. Huge SERP footprint advantage. Use on every service page with genuine Q&A.

AggregateRating

Shows star ratings in search results. Pull from real Google Reviews shown on-page with structured data.

BlogPosting

For blog content. Includes author, dates. Helps Google understand freshness and real authorship E-E-A-T signals.

BreadcrumbList

Shows hierarchy in SERP (Home › Services › Web Design). Improves navigation signals and CTR with better presentation.

Person

Your name, expertise, social links. For a solo agency, Google trusts a real expert over an anonymous business entity.

WebSite

Enables a search box directly in your Google listing. Add siteLinksSearchBox schema once you have brand recognition.

Implementation — JSON-LD (the correct method)

Add inside <script type="application/ld+json"> in your <head>. No plugin needed, works on any platform.

LocalBusiness Schema 
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Webresolv",
  "url": "https://webresolv.com",
  "telephone": "+91-XXXXXXXXXX",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "addressLocality": "Delhi",
    "addressCountry": "IN"
  },
  "openingHours": "Mo-Fr 09:00-18:00",
  "sameAs": ["https://linkedin.com/in/...", "https://instagram.com/webresolv"]
}
FAQPage Schema 
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "How much does web design cost in India?",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "Web design in India costs ₹15,000–₹2,00,000 depending on complexity..."
    }
  }]
}

Validate & test your schema

  • Rich Results Test search.google.com/test/rich-results — validates schema and previews how your rich result will look in SERPs. Run this every time you add schema.
  • Schema.org Validator validator.schema.org — checks for errors and warnings in your structured data properties.
  • Google Search Console Enhancements Shows rich result errors and which pages are affected. Check this monthly to catch broken schema early.
  • Merkle Schema Generator technicalseo.com/tools/schema-markup-generator — GUI tool for generating correct JSON-LD without hand-coding it.

Technical SEO ensures Google can actually find, crawl, understand, and index your site. You can write perfect content and it simply won't rank if the technical foundation is broken underneath it.

Core Web Vitals — Google's ranking factors

Loading Metric LCP

Largest Contentful Paint

How long until the largest element loads. Target: under 2.5 seconds. Fixed by optimising images, upgrading hosting, or using a CDN.

Interactivity Metric INP

Interaction to Next Paint

How fast the page responds to user interactions. Target: under 200ms. Caused by heavy JavaScript blocking the main thread.

Visual Stability Metric CLS

Cumulative Layout Shift

How much the page jumps as it loads. Target: under 0.1. Fix by specifying width and height on images and embeds.

Technical SEO master checklist

  • HTTPS (SSL) Non-negotiable. Google gives ranking credit and browsers warn users on HTTP sites. Free SSL via Let's Encrypt on any host.
  • Mobile-first indexing Google primarily indexes your mobile version. Test with Google's Mobile-Friendly Test. If your mobile site is bad, your rankings are bad everywhere.
  • XML Sitemap Submit to Google Search Console at yoursite.com/sitemap.xml. Lists all indexable pages. Regenerate whenever you add significant pages.
  • Robots.txt Block crawlers from admin pages, checkout, thank-you pages, login. Never accidentally block CSS/JS files — Google needs those to render your pages.
  • Canonical tags <link rel="canonical"> prevents duplicate content issues when the same content exists at multiple URLs (www/non-www, trailing slash, HTTP/HTTPS variants).
  • Image optimisation Use WebP format. Compress all images under 100KB. Lazy-load below-the-fold images. Name files descriptively: web-design-portfolio-delhi.webp not IMG_0043.jpg.
  • Page speed Target 90+ on PageSpeed Insights mobile. Enable caching, minify CSS/JS, use Cloudflare (free CDN), reduce TTFB under 200ms. Speed is now a direct ranking signal.
  • Fix broken links Run Screaming Frog quarterly. Find all 404 errors and either fix the links or add 301 redirects. Broken links waste crawl budget and hurt user experience.
  • Flat site architecture Every important page reachable in 3 clicks from homepage. Deeper pages get less crawl priority. Don't bury your best service pages under multiple levels of navigation.
  • 301 redirects Whenever you rename or delete a URL, add a permanent redirect to the new location. Chain of redirects (A→B→C) wastes crawl budget — make direct redirects (A→C).
  • Semantic HTML Proper heading hierarchy (H1→H2→H3), no skipped levels, descriptive nav labels. Clean markup helps crawlers and screen readers interpret your content structure.

Crawl budget tip: For sites under 500 pages it's rarely an issue. For larger sites, block filtered URLs, paginated archives, and admin paths via robots.txt so Google's crawl budget is spent on your revenue-generating pages, not pagination noise.

Google watches how users interact after clicking. High dwell time, low pogo-sticking, strong CTR — these tell Google your content satisfies search intent. Over time they directly push your rankings up.

Behavioural signals Google tracks

CTR

% who click your result. Higher CTR tells Google your title/description is the best answer. Low CTR pages lose rankings over time.

Dwell time

How long someone stays after clicking from Google. Longer = better. Users leaving in 5 seconds signals your content failed to satisfy the query.

Pogo-sticking

User clicks your result, bounces back to Google, and clicks a competitor. Hard negative signal. Fix by answering the query clearly and fast.

Return visits

Users coming back signals quality and trust. Email newsletters and strong content bring people back. Repeat visits improve brand authority signals.

How to increase CTR from SERPs

  • Numbers and brackets in titles "7 Web Design Trends in India [2025]" outperforms "Web Design Trends". Specificity signals quality to users scanning results.
  • Action CTAs in meta descriptions End with action phrases. "See our portfolio →" or "Get a free quote" increases clicks vs flat descriptions. Meta descriptions are your ad copy.
  • Rich results from schema Stars, FAQ dropdowns, breadcrumbs in your SERP listing double your visual footprint. Users click the bigger result. This is the fastest CTR win available.
  • Show freshness "Updated June 2025" in the title or meta increases CTR because users trust recent results over undated content, especially for "best" and "top" queries.
  • A/B test your titles In GSC, find pages with position 5–15 but low CTR. Rewrite the title tag and watch CTR change over 2–4 weeks. It's free rank movement.

Reducing bounce rate and increasing dwell time

  • Answer the query in the first 2–3 sentences. Don't warm up for 200 words. Users instantly hit back if they don't see the answer they came for.
  • Add a table of contents with jump links on long content. Encourages exploration and users staying in the page instead of bouncing.
  • Embed videos and interactive elements. Even a 2-minute explainer adds 2+ minutes of dwell time. Google treats video-enriched pages as higher quality.
  • Contextual internal links — "You might also like" or inline text links keep users moving through your site. The longer they stay in your ecosystem, the stronger your signals.
  • Match content format to query intent. "Best design tools" searchers want a scannable list, not an essay. If page 1 is all listicles, write a listicle, not a long-form guide.

Featured snippets (Position Zero): Get ~8% CTR even above position 1. To target them: find queries you rank positions 4–10 for, add a concise direct answer (40–60 words) immediately after an H2 question heading, use structured formats — numbered lists, tables, short definitions. Google pulls snippets from well-structured, clearly-answered content.

E-E-A-T trust signals: Build it via author bios with credentials, real client results and case studies, mentions from industry publications, a photo of you on the About page, published Google Reviews, and a clear privacy policy + contact page. This is Google's trust framework for ranking decisions.

Keyword strategy built for leads, not just traffic

"[service] + [city]" targets. "Web design agency Delhi", "SEO services Noida". Create location pages with unique content.

Pricing keywords

Comparison

"How to hire"

Converting SEO traffic into actual leads

  • CTA above the fold on service pages "Get a free consultation" or "Request a quote" form visible without any scrolling. If users have to scroll to find contact, many won't bother.
  • Lead magnets on blog posts "Download our free website audit checklist" in exchange for email. Captures visitors who aren't ready to hire yet. Nurture them via email.
  • WhatsApp chat button A sticky WhatsApp button on your site converts hesitant visitors. Indian clients strongly prefer WhatsApp over forms. Tawk.to or a simple wa.me link works.
  • Social proof above the fold Client logos, Google Review stars, "50+ projects delivered". Reduces friction before users even read your content. Trust signals convert.
  • Google Business Profile (GBP) Essential for local SEO. Fully optimise your GBP listing with services, photos, regular posts, and Q&A. Drives calls and Map conversions.
  • Dedicated landing pages Don't send all SEO traffic to home page. Create /web-design-delhi, /seo-services-delhi, /digital-marketing-noida. Targets specific cluster & CTA.
  • Blog → service CTA End every blog post with a relevant CTA. "Need a website built? Let's talk." Informational traffic converts when you guide it.

Local SEO for consistent lead flow

  • Fully complete GBP — every field, every service category, 10+ photos, weekly posts, respond to reviews within 24 hours
  • Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across website, GBP, Justdial, Sulekha, IndiaMART, Clutch.co, and all industry directories
  • Actively collect Google Reviews — ask every happy client directly. Reviews directly determine Local Pack (map results) ranking position
  • Create a location page for each area you serve with unique content per page (Delhi, Noida, Gurugram, etc.) — don't use the same copy
  • Embed Google Maps on your contact page — signals location relevance to Google's local ranking algorithm

Free tools — use these before anything else

Google Search Console (GSC)

Free

Role: Shows queries your site ranks for, impressions, clicks, CTR, crawl errors, Core Web Vitals issues, indexing status, and manual penalties.

Weekly use: find pages with position 5–15 and low CTR — rewrite their titles. Find crawl errors — fix them. Submit new pages for faster indexing.

Google Analytics 4 (GA4)

Free

Role: All user behaviour — traffic sources, page performance, session duration, conversion events.

Critical setup: configure conversion events for form submissions, WhatsApp clicks. Identify which SEO pages convert to enquiries vs traffic vanity metrics.

PageSpeed Insights

Free

Role: Audits Core Web Vitals (LCP, INP, CLS), TTFB, and overall performance. Provides specific fix recommendations with severity ratings.

Run it: after every major site change and monthly to catch regressions. Target 90+ mobile and desktop separately.

Rich Results Test

Free

Role: Validates schema markup and previews how rich results (FAQ, stars, breadcrumbs) appear in SERPs.

Run it: every time you add or change schema on any page. Also surfaces errors blocking your rich results from showing.

Freemium / paid tools

Ubersuggest

Freemium

Role: Keyword research (volume, KD, CPC), competitor analysis, backlink checker, site audit. Free tier: 3 searches/day. Best for: quick keyword validation and seeing what competitors rank for.

Ahrefs

Paid ~$99/mo

Role: Best backlink analysis in the industry, keyword research with click data, rank tracking, site audit, content gap analysis, competitor intelligence. Best for: building your backlink strategy.

SEMrush

Paid ~$120/mo

Role: Stronger than Ahrefs on competitor research, PPC/paid search data, content marketing, and social monitoring. Better all-in-one if you run ads. Pick one paid tool, do not buy both.

Freemium

Minimal free stack to start: Google Search Console + GA4 + Ubersuggest + Screaming Frog (free) covers 80% of your SEO needs at zero cost. Add Ahrefs/SEMrush once you're managing multiple clients and need volume or deep competitor intelligence.

Backlinks are votes of confidence from other websites. One link from a high-authority relevant site tells Google more about your trustworthiness than 1,000 low-quality links. Quality is the only thing that matters here.

DO — White hat tactics

  • Guest posting Write genuinely useful articles for real industry blogs (design, startup, SMB). Contextual link from real editorial content. Only on sites with actual audiences.
  • HARO (Help A Reporter Out) Respond to journalist queries on web design/digital marketing. Get quoted with a backlink from news sites. Completely free, high DA links.
  • Resource page link building Find "top web design resources" pages and pitch your content for inclusion. Email the owner with a genuine value prop.
  • Broken link building Find broken outbound links on relevant sites via Ahrefs. Create content that replaces the dead resource. Email the site owner — they'll thank you.
  • Client backlinks Ask clients to link to you in their site footer or "Built by" section. Real contextual links from legitimate business sites.
  • Quality directories Clutch.co, GoodFirms, DesignRush, Justdial, IndiaMART, Crunchbase, LinkedIn company page. Relevant and trusted citations.
  • Create linkable assets Original research, free tools, comprehensive guides, industry data. Content so useful others naturally link to it without you asking.
  • Digital PR Publish a data study or take a strong public position. Pitch to tech journalists and business publications. High DA editorial links.
  • Podcast appearances Appear on startup/business/design podcasts. Show notes almost always include a link to your site.

DON'T — Black / grey hat

  • Buying links Fiverr backlink packages, "DA 50+ links for $10" services. Google's spam algorithm detects purchased link patterns. Manual penalty = site removed.
  • Private Blog Networks (PBNs) Links from networks of fake sites. Google deindexes entire PBN networks regularly. Catastrophic and permanent when caught.
  • Comment spam Dropping links in blog comments. These are nofollow (zero SEO value) and mark you as a spammer with the platform and with Google.
  • Link farms / web rings Groups of sites that exist only to link to each other. Google has been algorithmically devaluing these since Penguin 2012.
  • Exact-match anchor stuffing Every backlink using "web design agency Delhi" is unnatural. Vary: brand name, bare URL, generic, descriptive phrases.
  • Mass directory blasts Submitting to 500 low-quality directories via automated tools. Low-authority irrelevant citations are ignored or hurt you.
  • Reciprocal schemes Systematic "I'll link to you if you link to me" reciprocal link structures at scale. Exchange programmes trigger spam filters.
  • Thin guest posts Churning out 300-word articles just to get a link placed. Google flags these as manipulative link schemes. Guest post where you'd be proud of the work.

Backlink quality metrics to check before outreach

Domain Rating / DA

Ahrefs DR or Moz DA. Target links from sites DR/DA 30+. One DR 70 link beats 100 DR 10 links. The higher the better.

Topical relevance

A link from a web design blog is worth 5× more than a link from a travel site, even at the same DR. Relevance multiplies authority.

Organic traffic

The linking site should have real organic traffic. Check in Ahrefs. Sites with zero traffic may be deindexed or link farms. Traffic = trust.

Dofollow / Nofollow

Dofollow passes ranking authority. Nofollow (social, forums) doesn't directly — but still drives real traffic and brand mentions.

Disavow toxic links: If you've ever bought links or received spammy links, use Google's Disavow Tool in Search Console. Only do this if you have clear evidence of a spam pattern — incorrectly disavowing good links hurts rankings. Use Ahrefs' toxicity checker first to identify genuinely harmful links before disavowing anything.

How to Create Blogs — Step-by-Step Workflow

  • 1

    Keyword & Intent Selection

    Choose one primary target keyword with low KD (under 30) and clear commercial/transactional intent. Map out 3-5 LSI terms.

  • 2

  • 4

  • 6

    Checklist Validation

    Verify optimization using the Rank Math guidelines, aiming for a green SEO score (80+) before hitting publish.

Best Approach — Blog Structure & Silo Strategy

SEO Title & H1 Hook Use the focus keyword near the start. Increase CTR by including specific numbers, brackets, and target search dates (e.g. "Old vs New Tax Regime Guide [June 2026]"). Only one H1 per post.
Search Date & Freshness Always state a "Last Updated / Reviewed" date at the top of the blog content. This satisfies search date queries and signals to Google's crawlers that the page is maintained. Ensure the visible date aligns with your schema's dateModified property.
URL Slug Optimization Keep URL slugs clean, lowercase, and hyphenated containing only the focus keyword (e.g., /blog/old-vs-new-regime-2026). Do not include categories, dates, or numbers unless part of the keyword. Avoid changing slugs post-launch.
Silo Architecture (Categories & Tags) Group posts logically under parent Categories (e.g. "Tax Planning"). Use Tags (e.g. "ITR Filing", "Form 16") to capture granular topics. Internal link articles in the same category (silo linking) to establish topical relevance.

Dos & Don'ts of Blog Creation

DO — Standard requirements

  • First 100 words Naturally include the target focus keyword in the opening paragraph. Answer the search query early.
  • Image Alt Text Write Alt text for all images, incorporating secondary/LSI keywords. Keep descriptions natural.
  • Descriptive Anchors Link internally using natural context terms. Link "old vs new tax regime table" instead of "click here".
  • Call to Actions Include a relevant, high-converting CTA (such as email magnet or WhatsApp inquiry link) at the end of each post.

DON'T — Spam & errors to avoid

  • Keyword stuffing Do not repeat keywords obsessively. Keep keyword density under 1.5% to avoid search classification penalties.
  • Raw AI generation Do not copy-paste raw ChatGPT output. Google's Helpful Content System penalises low-value, unedited AI content.
  • Slug modifications Never change a slug once published without adding a 301 redirect. Breaking the link destroys existing indexing equity.
  • Keyword cannibalization Avoid optimizing multiple posts for the exact same focus keyword. Create a keyword map to keep them distinct.
